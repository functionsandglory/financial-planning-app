# Charity Integration Ideas

*Feature concepts for financial planning tool - April 1, 2026*

---

## 1. Charity as a Core Entity

Treat charitable giving as a first-class citizen alongside accounts, income, and expenses.

**Donor-Advised Funds (DAF)**
- Account type with tax-advantaged growth
- Contribute now, donate later strategy
- Tax deduction timing separate from grant timing

**Recurring Donations**
- Scheduled events (monthly, quarterly, annual)
- Tied to income events ("give 10% of each paycheck")
- Annual giving goals with progress tracking

**Legacy Bequests**
- Planned giving in estate/retirement timeline
- Charitable remainder trusts as account type
- Automatic will/trust integration reminders

**Impact Tracking**
- "Your $500/month = 50 meals/year"
- Auto-calculated based on charity efficiency ratings
- Visual progress toward specific impact goals

---

## 2. The "Values Branch"

Use the Git-style branching to model giving scenarios.

**Comparison Scenarios**
- Branch: "Retire at 60 with 5% giving" vs. "Retire at 65 with 15% giving"
- Side-by-side net worth AND impact visualization
- See both the cost and the benefit of generosity

**The "What If" Questions**
- What if I gave 10% vs 20% of income?
- What if I donated appreciated stock instead of cash?
- What if I front-load giving in early retirement?

**Visual Impact**
- Net worth line + "lives impacted" counter
- Branch comparison shows: "I have $200K less, but I funded 40 scholarships"

---

## 3. Tax Optimization Engine

Make tax savings from charity a core feature, not an afterthought.

**Automated Suggestions**
- "Donate appreciated stock instead of cash → Save $X in taxes → Redirect to more giving"
- "Bunch 2 years of donations into one tax year → Itemize deductions → Save $Y"

**Real-Time Tax Savings**
- Show tax benefit of each donation as you enter it
- "This $1,000 donation saves you $240 in taxes"
- Net cost vs. gross cost display

**Strategy Comparison**
- Cash vs. stock vs. DAF
- Annual vs. bunched giving
- Tax year timing optimization

---

## 4. Charity Efficiency Scores

Help users give smarter, not just more.

**Data Integration**
- Charity Navigator ratings
- GuideStar financial data
- Cause IQ efficiency metrics

**Impact Per Dollar**
- "Charity A: 90¢ of every $1 goes to programs"
- "Charity B: 75¢ goes to programs"
- Warning: "This charity spends 40% on fundraising"

**Alternative Suggestions**
- "You're giving to Charity X. Charity Y achieves similar outcomes with 30% less overhead."
- Efficiency-adjusted impact calculations

---

## 5. "Giving Budget" Equal to Savings

Elevate charitable giving in the UI and mental model.

**Primary Goal Status**
- Progress bar: "Retirement: 75% funded"
- Progress bar: "Giving Fund: 40% funded"
- Equal visual weight, equal importance

**The Philosophy**
- You can't say you value education if it's not in the budget
- Giving is not "what's left over" — it's a primary allocation
- Default assumption: Everyone has giving goals

**Required Field**
- Can't complete financial plan without addressing giving
- Skip option with prompt: "Are you sure? You can add this later."

---

## 6. Legacy Timeline

Extend planning beyond death.

**Multi-Generational View**
- Timeline extends to 100+ years
- Show: Annual giving while alive
- Show: Bequest at death
- Show: Perpetual foundation or endowment

**Your Impact Continues**
- "Year 2075: Your scholarship fund supports student #50"
- "Year 2100: Your bequest has compounded to $X, granting $Y annually"

**Visual Design**
- Your life in color, post-life impact in softer tone
- Ancestor/descendant timeline view

---

## 7. Values Alignment Score

Hold users accountable to their stated values.

**The Prompt**
- "What causes matter to you?" (Education, Health, Environment, Arts, etc.)
- Rank 3-5 priorities

**The Reality Check**
- "You say education matters to you, but 2% of spending supports it"
- "Your giving aligns 40% with your stated values"

**Rebalancing Suggestions**
- "Shift $200/month from dining to scholarship fund?"
- "Your health charity supports research, but direct aid has higher impact. Consider Charity Y."

---

## Implementation Approach

**Phase 1: Core Giving**
- DAF account type
- Recurring donation events
- Basic tax optimization (cash vs. stock)

**Phase 2: Intelligence**
- Charity efficiency scores
- Tax optimization engine
- Values alignment tracking

**Phase 3: Legacy**
- Bequest planning
- Multi-generational timeline
- Perpetual foundation modeling

**UI Concept:**
Add a **"Giving" tab** equal to "Investments" and "Retirement." Make it impossible to build a complete plan without addressing charitable goals.

---

*The Goal:* Shift financial planning from pure wealth accumulation to values-based stewardship. Help people give more, give smarter, and align their money with their principles.
